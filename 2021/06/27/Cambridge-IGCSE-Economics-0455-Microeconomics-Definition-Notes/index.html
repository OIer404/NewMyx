Cambridge IGCSE Economics 0455 Microeconomics Definition Notes

Definitions

  • Factors of production

    Resources used to produce goods and services, including capital, enterprise, labour and land.

  • Land
    Natural resources, such as coal and oil.

  • Labour
    Human resources, which is use people’s hands and brains to help make good and services.

  • Capital
    Man-made resources, which help to produce many other goods and services.

  • Enterprise
    Risk-taking ability and willingness to run business.

  • Entrepreneur
    The people who have enterprise and can control and manage firms.
    Take risk and making decisions.

  • Opportunity cost
    The next best option that we give up.

  • Free goods
    No opportunity cost when consuming free goods.

  • Economic goods
    The consumption of economic goods involved opportunity cost.

  • Utility
    The level of happiness or satisfaction arising from consuming goods.

  • Production Possibility Curve
    It shows the combined maximum possible output of 2 goods can be produced with existing resources and technology.

  • Economic System
    An economic system determines how scarce resources are used.
    Resources allocation

  • Substitute
    Can satisfy the same wants, such as taxi&bus.

  • Complement
    A good consumed with another have joint demand, such as car&patrol.

  • Market force
    Market force is an interaction between supply of goods and demand for goods in a market.

  • Demand
    The willingness and ability to buy the product at any given price.

  • Supply
    The willingness and ability of firms to make and sell a product at any given price.

  • Subsidy
    Financial support firm government to encourage production and consumption.

  • Price Elasticity of Demand
    The responsiveness of quantity demanded to a change in price.

  • Price Elasticity of Supply
    The responsiveness of quantity supplied to a change in price.

  • Revenue
    The amount of money that collected from the consumers.

  • Externality
    It is effect on the third party(someone not directly involved).
    Spillover effect

  • Private cost
    Payment for goods and services.

  • Private benefit
    Satisfaction from consuming the products the buy.

  • Excludability
    It is possible to exclude some people from using a private good.
    It is usually done by charging a price.

  • Rival
    Competing for use of something.

  • Public good
    Non-rival and non-excludable, such as defense.

  • Private good
    Rival and excludable, such as restaurant.

  • Market failure
    It happens when market fails to allocate resources efficiently.

  • Unemployment
    It happens when a person is willing and able to work, actively looking for job but couldn’t find.

  • Underemployment
    Labour or other factors and production and not fully utilized.

  • Merit good
    A good which is under-provided by the market mechanism because its external benefits are not known or appreciated by consumers(information failure).

  • Demerit good
    A good which is over-provided by the market mechanism because consumers overvalue the goods and lack the information of its negative effects.

  • Indirect tax
    A tax on consumers’ expenditure/spending to reduce production&consumption

  • Regressive
    As income rises, a smaller proportion of income is paid as tax.

  • Progressive
    As income rises, a larger proportion of income is paid as tax.

  • Proportional
    As income rises, som proportional of income is paid as tax.

  • Barter
    Barter system is exchange one good/service for another without using money.

  • Bank
    It is a financial intermediary, it brings together consumer who want to save money and customers who want to borrow it.

  • Spending pattern
    The proportion of disposable income individuals spend and what they spend it on.

  • Industrial sector
    A group of firms specializing in similar goods and hour services.

  • Primary sector
    The extraction and production of natural resources. Like farming, forestry, mining.

  • Secondary sector
    Manufacturing and construction.

  • Tertiary sector(Service sector)
    Provides services. Like banking, insurance, retailing.

  • Trade union
    It is an organisation of workers formed to protect the interests of their members concerning wage rate, benefits and working conditions.

  • Collective bargaining
    Negotiations between trade union and employers improvement in wages and other non-wage benefits for employees.

  • Productivity
    It is the output produced per factor/input over a given period of time and it measures how efficiently resources are used in production.

  • Internal growth
    Firms expand its scale of production through purchasing more equipment, increasing the size of its premises(shops/offices/factories)and hiring more labour.

  • External growth
    Two or more firms join together to form a larger firm or integration.

  • Merger
    A merger occurs when two or more firms combined together to form a new large company.

  • Takeover/Acquisition
    One company acquires another company and takes control.

  • Horizontal integration
    It occurs between firms producing the same type of good or service.

  • Vertical integration
    It occurs between firms at different stages& production.

  • Lateral integration
    It occurs between firms in different industries.

  • Economies of scale
    As the firm is expanding its production scale, the average cost would fall.

  • Diseconomies of scale
    As the firm is over-expanding its production scale, the average cost will rise again.

  • Internal economies of scale
    Lower average cost when the size of a firm increases.

  • External economies of scale
    These are cost savings enjoyed by firms in large industries.

  • Fixed cost
    The costs that do not vary with the level of output, such as the rents.

  • Total fixed cost
    The sum of all fixed costs.

  • Variable cost
    The costs vary directly with the level of the output, such as purchases of raw materials.

  • Total variable cost
    Variable costs✖️Output

  • Total cost
    Total fixed cost+Total variable cost

  • Average cost per unit of output
    Total cost/total output

  • Revenue
    Firms earn revenue or takeover form scale of their good and services to consumers.

  • Monopoly
    A monopoly is a single firm or small group of firms acting together that has sufficient power to restrict competition and determine market price. The barriers to entry the market is high.

  • Perfectly competitive market
    In a perfectly competitive market, there is a large number firms supplying identical good with very low barriers to entry. Firms in a perfectly competitive market are all price takers.

  • Economy
    A system of activities involving the production, consumption and exchange of goods and services.

  • Equilibrium price
    When market demand is equal to market supply and there is no tendency to change.

  • Disequilibrium price
    When market supply is not equal to market supply and there is a tendency to change.

  • Labour-intensive
    Employ more labour than capital equipment and machinery.

  • Capital-intensive
    Employ more capital equipment and machinery and relatively few workers.

  • Labour productivity
    It measures the amount of output produced per labour.
